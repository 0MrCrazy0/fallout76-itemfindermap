# Fallout 76 Item Finder Map

**Live version:** [https://0mrcrazy0.github.io/fallout76-itemfindermap/](https://0mrcrazy0.github.io/fallout76-itemfindermap/)

A simple, free, no-login browser-based map tool for Fallout 76 players to mark and track anything in Appalachia — magazines, bobbleheads, plans, treasure maps, junk nodes, power armor spawns, or any custom locations you discover.

### Features
- Right-click (PC) or long-press (mobile) anywhere on the map to open the **Choose Action** menu  
- Create permanent markers (automatically locked and glowing) or temporary postcards  
- Unlock markers to drag and reposition them, then lock when satisfied  
- Filter markers by category (default + your own custom categories)  
- All data saved locally in your browser — no account required  
- Export / Import your markers as JSON (share with friends or backup easily)  
- Download Community Map — periodically update to include markers submitted by other players  
- Earn XP and level up for every marker you log  
- Fully offline-capable after first load  
- Mobile-optimized

### How to Use
1. Open the live link: https://0mrcrazy0.github.io/fallout76-itemfindermap/  
2. Click **“How to Use”** in the tools panel for full instructions  
3. **PC:** Right-click the map → **Mobile:** Long-press the map to open the **Choose Action** menu  
4. Choose:  
   - **Create Permanent Marker** — to log a new item (locked automatically)  
   - **Create Temporary Postcard** — to share a temporary spot (expires in 5 minutes)  
5. Use the Search Bar to filter by description, category, or grid coordinates (e.g. “C4”)  
6. Click **“Update Community Map”** occasionally to receive the latest community discoveries  

Enjoy exploring Appalachia — and happy logging!

### FAQ

**Q: How do I create a marker on mobile?**  
A: Long-press anywhere on the map to open the Choose Action menu, then tap “Create Permanent Marker”.

**Q: What’s the difference between a permanent marker and a postcard?**  
A: Permanent markers stay forever and are locked by default. Postcards are temporary (expire after 5 minutes) and are meant for sharing short-term spots.

**Q: Why are some markers glowing green?**  
A: New markers glow for 2 minutes after being created or moved. They automatically lock after the glow fades.

**Q: How do I move a marker?**  
A: Unlock it first (tap the lock icon in the popup or edit modal), then drag it. It will re-lock automatically when you drop it.

**Q: Is my data safe and private?**  
A: Yes. Everything is stored only in your browser. No account or server upload is required unless you choose to submit a marker to the Community Map.

**Q: How do I backup my markers?**  
A: Use the “Export Markers” button in the tools panel. You can choose “Personal Only” or “Personal + Kept”.

**Q: What does the Community Map do?**  
A: The Community Map adds high-quality, hand-picked markers that have been submitted by players and reviewed/approved by moderators. All user-submitted markers are checked before they are added to the official community JSON file.  
You can click “Update Community Map” regularly to receive the latest verified discoveries. If you have kept a community marker and later find something wrong with it, you can revert it back to community status or report it directly from its popup.

**Q: Can I use the app on iPhone or Android?**  
A: Yes — the app is fully supported on both iPhone and Android. Recent updates have resolved all known iPhone touch and fullscreen issues, so the experience is now smooth and reliable on mobile devices.

**Q: How do I install the app as a PWA (Progressive Web App)?**  
A:  
- **iPhone (iOS):** Open the map in Safari → tap the Share button → scroll down and select “Add to Home Screen”.  
- **Android:** Open the map in Chrome → tap the three-dot menu → select “Add to Home Screen” or “Install app”.  
- **PC (Chrome/Edge):** Click the Install icon in the address bar (or go to the three-dot menu → “Install Fallout 76 Item Finder Map”).  

Once installed, the app will appear as a standalone icon on your home screen or desktop and work even better offline.
